The switching that hurts

It isn't between AI tools. It's between AI and the tools where work is stored: Jira, Confluence, Drive, Miro, analytics. Task lists are the most scattered of all.

"Jira for devs, but also a todo list in my monster folder" — R8
"Nothing beats a physical notebook for me" — R11
Theme 16 of 11 said this directly

If a tool isn't connected, its context is lost

What gets lost when moving between tools? For connected tools, nothing. For everything else, everything.

What a question. Everything that's not connected to Notion or Google?

R2 · Founder, Operations
context around the thing – whatever I'm not asking AI or it doesn't infer might also be useful gets lost unless I've thought about telling it it's important
R7 · CMO
Currently my issue is moving data from some analytics tools without MCP connectors. When you move mix panel user data, you cant filter videos with the csv having the user id with MSclarity and that is so limiting.

Implication

People aren't describing a prompting problem. They're describing a plumbing problem. What gets lost is the background stuff you didn't think to paste in, and no amount of extra effort brings it back reliably.

What this points to

Three takeaways

01

Connections matter most

Both kinds of loss happen at the edges. The value comes from how many tools connect in, and how well results are written back out, not from smarter answers.

02

The DIY builders are the first users

10 of 11 already built their own systems and know where they fall short. They have the problem, the words for it, and setups ready to move over.

03

Planning is the #1 unsolved job

The biggest, most-mentioned gap: pulling tickets, requests and notes from many places into a clear plan. It always crosses tools.
